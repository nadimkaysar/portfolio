Short Bio

I am a Ph.D. student in the Computer Science & Engineering Department of the Nagoya Institute of Technology. I have earned Bachelor of Science degrees in Computer Science & Engineering from the World University of Bangladesh and obtained my Master degree in Computer Science from Nagoya Institute of Technology, Japan.

My research focuses on LLM-based dialogue systems, machine learning, and data analysis, emphasizing mental health support frameworks. I work on cognitive analysis, machine translation, sequence classification, and social media analysis for public health issues, including depression and mental health, with applications across social, biological, and medical domains.

Research Interest

Natural Language Processing, Chatbot, Dialogue System, Large Language Models, Machine Learning, Healthcare, Health Informatics, Data Analysis, Decision Making, Human Behavior Analysis, Mental Health, Classification, Cognitive Analysis, and Machine Translation.
